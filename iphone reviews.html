Choosing Your Next iPhone

The current lineup of smartphones offers a wide variety of choices, ranging from the compact models to the massive Pro Max editions. When reviewing these devices, we look closely at the camera capabilities and the A-series chip efficiency. It is important to note that most users do not need the highest-end model to get a premium experience. Often, the base model or previous year's Pro model offers the best value for your money. We recommend assessing your primary usage—such as photography, gaming, or general social media—before making a final purchase.

Battery life remains one of the most critical factors for any mobile user. In our testing, the Pro Max models consistently lead the pack, often lasting well over a full day of heavy use. However, the standard models have seen significant improvements in efficiency, making them more reliable than ever before. If you are a power user who records a lot of 4K video, you should definitely consider the higher storage tiers. ProRes video and high-resolution photos can fill up a 128GB phone faster than you might expect, so plan accordingly for your storage needs.
